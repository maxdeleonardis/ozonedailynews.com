## What OzoneNews Is

OzoneNews is a **verified digital news platform** built on Next.js 15, React 19, and Tailwind CSS, delivering high-quality journalism across technology, gaming, finance, entertainment, and breaking news. We are **not** a blog, content aggregator, or opinion site. Every article we publish must be verifiable, sourced from primary materials, and written to serve real search intent.

### Our Editorial Foundation

1. **Accuracy Over Speed** — We verify before publishing. We never chase breaking news without proper sourcing. If we can't confirm it, we don't publish it.

2. **Primary Sources Only** — We cite origins, not aggregators. Every claim must be traceable to its original source. When reporting on company announcements, we link to the official press release or SEC filing, not to another news site's coverage.

3. **Transparent Corrections** — Errors are corrected publicly, timestamped, with the original text preserved. Our corrections policy is linked from every page footer.

4. **Clear Separation** — News articles and opinion pieces are clearly labeled. Sponsored content is disclosed with mandatory "Sponsored" badges. No undisclosed affiliate links.

5. **Named Authors** — All articles have bylines with valid author slugs linking to author profile pages. Authors must have an external footprint (Twitter or LinkedIn) or use the "OzoneNews Editorial Team" house byline.

## Verification Standards

Every article published on OzoneNews must meet these verification requirements:

### Tier 1 Sources (Primary — Always Acceptable)
- Official company press releases
- SEC filings (8-K, 10-K, 10-Q, proxy statements)
- Government agency announcements (FDA, FTC, DOJ, FCC)
- Earnings call transcripts (verified via SeekingAlpha, company IR sites)
- Academic research papers (peer-reviewed journals)
- Official developer blogs, patch notes, technical documentation
- Court documents (dockets, rulings, filings from PACER or court websites)

### Tier 2 Sources (Secondary — Acceptable with Cross-Verification)
- Major news wire services (Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, WSJ)
- Industry trade publications (TechCrunch for funding rounds, Variety for entertainment deals)
- Verified social media accounts (company accounts, CEO/founder accounts with blue checks)
- Data aggregators with transparent methodology (SteamDB, Circana/NPD, comScore)

### Tier 3 Sources (Tertiary — Use Sparingly, Always Attribute)
- Anonymous tipsters (must have 2+ independent confirmations)
- Leaked documents (must verify authenticity via multiple checks)
- Reddit/forum posts (only when corroborated by Tier 1/2 sources)

### Banned Sources
- Content farms (no sourcing from sites that republish press releases without verification)
- Unverified social media rumors
- AI-generated content without human fact-checking
- Paywalled reports we haven't personally reviewed

## Technical Infrastructure

### Publishing Pipeline
1. **Content Creation** — Articles written as static JSON files in `content/static/` (on-prem storage, no external CMS)
2. **Metadata Validation** — `scripts/audit-metadata.ts` enforces:
   - Title ≤ 60 chars (no brand suffix)
   - Description 130-155 chars
   - No em dashes anywhere (banned by OStandard)
   - Mandatory OpenGraph images with dimensions
3. **Registry Sync** — `scripts/sync-registry.ts` builds `content_registry.json` from all `page.tsx` files
4. **Build Validation** — Prebuild hooks run 4 validators:
   - `validate-public.ts` — Blocks conflicting static files in `/public`
   - `validate-canonicals.ts` — Blocks hardcoded canonical tags in layouts
   - `validate-eeat.ts` — Checks E-E-A-T compliance (author footprint, description length)
   - `sync-registry.ts --write` — Updates content registry
5. **Deployment** — Railway auto-deploys on GitHub push to `main`

### SEO Architecture
- **Sitemap:** `app/sitemap.ts` — 720+ entries, updated dynamically
- **News Sitemap:** `app/news-sitemap.xml/route.ts` — 48-hour freshness window, 1,000 URL cap, full Google News protocol compliance
- **RSS Feed:** `app/rss.xml/route.ts` — Full-text articles, proper `<enclosure>` tags for images
- **Structured Data:** Every article has NewsArticle schema with:
  - Author as Person object (not string) with external `sameAs` URLs
  - Full ISO 8601 timestamps with timezone offsets
  - NewsMediaOrganization schema with trust page links
- **OG Images:** 164+ articles have Satori-generated 1200×675 OG images (auto-detected by registry sync)

### Trust Pages (Mandatory Google News Requirements)
All four trust pages are linked in the global footer and have dedicated URLs:
1. **About** (`/about`) — Company background, mission, founding story
2. **Editorial Standards** (`/editorial-standards`) — Publishing principles, verification process, sourcing rules
3. **Corrections Policy** (`/corrections`) — How we handle errors, correction request form
4. **Contact** (`/contact`) — Email, tip submission form, editorial inquiries

### Author Profiles
Every byline author has a `/authors/[slug]` page with:
- Full bio with career background
- External profile links (Twitter or LinkedIn — mandatory for non-house bylines)
- Article archive (all articles by that author)
- Author schema as Person object with `sameAs` URLs

## Post-March 2026 Core Update Compliance

OzoneNews fully complies with Google's March 2026 Core Update requirements for news sites:

### 1. Information Gain Requirement
Every article must contain **at least one** of the following:
- Original quote from a primary source (not aggregated from another news site)
- Contrarian take or unique analysis not found elsewhere
- Custom chart, screenshot, or data visualization
- Hand-written "Why This Matters" or "Context" section

**Example:** Our *Instagram Drops Encrypted DMs* article includes:
- 8 unique external sources (not just one press release)
- Custom analysis of Take It Down Act compliance timeline
- "Why This Matters" section explaining AI training data access implications

### 2. Banned LLM Transition Phrases
Our build pipeline automatically fails if any of these generic AI phrases appear:
- "In conclusion"
- "It is important to note"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "In the ever-evolving landscape"
- "Moreover, it should be noted"
- "Navigating the complex"
- "Delve into"

These are checked by `scripts/validate-eeat.ts` and will block deployment.

### 3. Author Footprint Validation
- Every author must have an external `sameAs` profile (Twitter or LinkedIn) on their `/authors/[slug]` page
- Authors with no external footprint must use the "OzoneNews Editorial Team" house byline
- Author schema is always a Person object, never a string
- Validated at build time by E-E-A-T checker

### 4. Timestamp Requirements
- All `publishedTime` and `modifiedTime` fields use full ISO 8601 with timezone offset: `2026-05-18T18:00:00-05:00`
- Date-only formats are banned (e.g., no `2026-05-18`)
- Timezone is always explicit (usually America/Chicago)

### 5. News Sitemap Restrictions
- Only articles published within the last **48 hours** appear in the news sitemap
- Maximum **1,000 URLs** (Google News limit)
- Articles older than 48h fall off the news sitemap but stay in the main sitemap

### 6. NewsMediaOrganization Schema
Lives in `app/layout.tsx` and includes links to all four trust pages:
- `publishingPrinciples` → `/editorial-standards`
- `correctionsPolicy` → `/corrections`
- `actionableFeedbackPolicy` → `/contact`
- `ethicsPolicy` → `/editorial-standards`

All four links are also visible as blue underlined links in the global footer under "Newsroom Policies".
