**Network:** Ozone Network News LLC (ONN)
**Main site:** `ozonenetwork.news` (this repo — covers everything)

---

## The Network Model

Google's March 2026 Core Update penalizes broad generalist sites and rewards topical authority. The ONN network answer is a hub-and-spoke model: one flagship that covers all news, surrounded by niche sub-brands that own a single vertical so deeply that Google treats each domain as the definitive source for that topic.

```
ozonenetwork.news (flagship — all verticals, aggregates sub-brand content)
    ├── basilnews.com        Food, restaurants, agriculture, food tech
    ├── clovernews.com       Personal finance, deals, consumer money
    ├── grovenews.com        Local, community, real estate, cities
    ├── sagenews.com         Health, wellness, longevity, biohacking
    ├── halonews.com         Cybersecurity, privacy, digital safety
    ├── statusnews.com       Business, luxury, executive, markets
    ├── micanews.com         Science, climate, environment, research
    └── onyxnews.com         Luxury lifestyle, culture, fashion, entertainment
```

Cross-linking rule: every sub-brand article links back to the relevant flagship page on `ozonenetwork.news`. Every flagship article in a covered vertical links out to the sub-brand. This passes authority bidirectionally.

## Network Cross-Linking Strategy

Every sub-brand article must follow these linking rules to pass authority across the network:

| Link Type | Rule |
|---|---|
| Sub-brand → Flagship | Every article links to the relevant hub page on `ozonenetwork.news` within the first 3 paragraphs |
| Flagship → Sub-brand | Every flagship article in a covered vertical links to the sub-brand in the related articles sidebar |
| Sub-brand → Sub-brand | When topics overlap (e.g. SageNews + MicaNews on a climate-health story), cross-link once per article |
| Author pages | All sub-brand authors have pages on `ozonenetwork.news/authors/[slug]` — sub-brand bylines link there |

## Launch Sequence

Do not launch all eight at once. Each domain needs at minimum 15 published articles before it is indexed meaningfully. Sequence by SEO opportunity and editorial bandwidth:

| Phase | Sites | Target Launch | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | `sagenews.com`, `halonews.com` | June 2026 | Health and cybersecurity have the highest search volume and lowest quality competition |
| Phase 2 | `statusnews.com`, `clovernews.com` | July 2026 | Business and personal finance are high-CPC verticals that monetize immediately |
| Phase 3 | `basilnews.com`, `onyxnews.com` | August 2026 | Food and luxury are strong brand-building verticals with loyal audiences |
| Phase 4 | `micanews.com`, `grovenews.com` | September 2026 | Science and local news require more editorial investment to do correctly |

**Pre-launch checklist for each domain:**
- [ ] 15+ published articles in the static JSON store before domain goes live
- [ ] All articles have `published_at` ISO-8601 timestamps
- [ ] `content_registry.json` fully populated
- [ ] `ozonenetwork.news` hub page linking to sub-brand exists
- [ ] Google Search Console property created and sitemap submitted
- [ ] `NewsMediaOrganization` schema live with all 4 trust page URLs
- [ ] Author profiles with real `sameAs` external URLs set in `lib/author-profiles.ts`
- [ ] `lifecycle: "news"` field on all fresh articles

## Content Reuse Policy

Sub-brand articles can reference or contextualize ozonenetwork.news stories but must NOT duplicate the same text. Each sub-brand article must provide at minimum one additional original element not in the flagship version:

- A different angle or audience frame
- One additional sourced statistic or quote
- A "What This Means for [niche audience]" section
- A niche-specific data table or timeline

This satisfies Google's information gain requirement and prevents self-cannibalization.

## Shared Infrastructure

All sub-brands run on the same Next.js 15 stack, same static JSON content model, same `NewsArticleDB` / `JackArticleDB` / `ArticlePageDB` components. A new sub-brand is a new Railway service pointing at a new domain with a new `content/static/` directory.

Shared across all sites:
- `components/` — all article, engagement, nav, and UI components
- `lib/` — `article-service.ts`, `registry-service.ts`, `seo-utils.ts`, `author-profiles.ts`
- `scripts/` — `lifecycle-manager.ts`, `sync-registry.ts`, `validate-canonicals.ts`
- `SITE_CONFIG` — override per domain via env var `NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_NAME`, `NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL`
