## Editorial Identity

OzoneNews covers the physical infrastructure of the future: the hardware, energy, science, and capital that determine what the next decade of technology can actually do. We report from primary sources. We do not aggregate, editorialize without named sourcing, or publish speculation as fact. Our reader is technically literate and time-constrained. Every article answers a specific question with verified data.

---

## Daily Coverage Mandate

Every publishing day OzoneNews targets a minimum of **3-5 original reported articles** across the active verticals below. Each article must have a primary source published the same day or within the prior 24 hours. Breaking events are published immediately on confirmation.

---

## What We Write About Every Day

### 1. Science (Daily — Highest Priority)

Peer-reviewed publications, space agency announcements, and confirmed observational discoveries. Primary sources: *Science*, *Nature*, *The Astrophysical Journal Letters*, *PNAS*, *Cell*, *PLOS Biology*, NOAA, NASA, ESA, EurekAlert.

**Daily monitoring list:**
- **Space weather** — NOAA SWPC Kp index, solar flare classifications, geomagnetic storm warnings, aurora events
- **Astrophysics** — ALMA, Chandra, Hubble, JWST, Event Horizon Telescope new findings
- **Planetary science / Mars** — Curiosity and Perseverance updates, orbital mission status, landing windows
- **NASA programs** — Artemis, Commercial Crew, Lunar Gateway, SLS/Orion, CLPS
- **Launch vehicles** — SpaceX Starship, Falcon 9, New Glenn, Vulcan, RocketLab
- **Biology / animal cognition** — peer-reviewed studies on intelligence, navigation, behavior, ecology
- **Climate monitoring** — NOAA sea ice data, Keeling Curve CO2, IPCC updates, reef bleaching
- **Environmental science** — ozone layer, atmospheric chemistry, pollution measurements
- **Ocean science** — NOAA ocean heat content, Schmidt Ocean Institute expeditions, Argo float data, marine biology

---

### 2. Tech (Daily — Second Priority)

Technology journalism focused on the physical infrastructure and hardware layer of the AI and computing economy. Primary sources: company press releases, SEC filings, IEEE publications, analyst reports, GamersNexus, Tom's Hardware, AnandTech.

**Daily monitoring list:**
- **AI compute infrastructure** — data center groundbreakings, power procurement deals, GPU supply chain
- **NVIDIA** — Blackwell/Rubin/Feynman architecture, RTX consumer hardware, NVLink/NVSwitch, Isaac robotics platform
- **Semiconductor manufacturing** — TSMC yield reports, Intel foundry, Samsung node transitions, ASML EUV news
- **Quantum computing** — IBM Heron, Google Quantum AI, IonQ, Microsoft topological qubits, *Nature* qubit papers
- **Robotics and physical AI** — Figure, 1X, Boston Dynamics, Tesla Optimus, DARPA robotics programs, research papers
- **Advanced materials** — graphene transistors, 2D materials, silicon photonics, photonic computing
- **Cloud infrastructure** — Google, Azure, AWS capacity expansion, colocation vs. owned facility decisions
- **Consumer GPU/CPU** — major hardware reviews, spec confirmations, pricing and availability
- **Space technology** — launch vehicle hardware, Starlink constellation, reusable booster milestones

---

### 3. Energy Infrastructure (Daily — Growing Priority)

The power story underneath the AI infrastructure story. Every hyperscale data center article has an energy dimension. Primary sources: DOE, NRC, FERC, utility filings, *Nature Energy*, company press releases.

**Daily monitoring list:**
- **Nuclear / SMRs** — NRC licensing decisions, TerraPower, NuScale, Kairos Power, Oklo, Commonwealth Fusion milestones, tech company nuclear PPAs
- **Power grid** — grid operator (PJM, ERCOT, CAISO) interconnection queue data, AI load growth reports, denial statistics
- **Battery storage** — CATL, Form Energy, Ambri, utility-scale deployment announcements, grid storage records
- **Renewable energy** — offshore wind contracts, solar farm groundbreakings specifically tied to AI data center demand
- **Data center power** — energy intensity benchmarks, PUE improvements, heat recovery deployments, water usage disclosures

---

### 4. Finance (Daily — Third Priority)

Capital markets coverage of technology, space, and energy companies. Primary sources: SEC filings, Bloomberg, Reuters, official earnings releases, S-1 documents, analyst initiation reports.

**Daily monitoring list:**
- **IPOs** — S-1 filings, pricing, analyst ratings, lock-up events for tech, space, and energy companies
- **Earnings** — NVIDIA, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, SpaceX investor releases, nuclear startup funding rounds
- **Valuations** — major funding rounds, secondary market pricing for private space and energy companies
- **Macro** — Fed decisions and treasury yield moves as they affect growth tech, space, and nuclear capital markets

---

### 5. Space Commerce (2-3x Per Week)

The economics of the orbital economy. Extends space science into business and finance territory. Primary sources: FCC filings, ITU registrations, company press releases, launch manifests, analyst reports.

**Monitoring list:**
- **Satellite broadband** — Starlink financials, Amazon Kuiper deployment milestones, Eutelsat OneWeb competition
- **Launch contracts** — NASA CLPS awards, DoD launch contracts, commercial payload pricing
- **Orbital manufacturing** — space station programs (Axiom, Vast), microgravity manufacturing announcements
- **Space debris policy** — ITU coordination, FCC deorbit rules, active debris removal programs (Astroscale)
- **Asteroid and resource economics** — viable mission announcements, regulatory framework developments

---

### 6. News (Breaking / As Needed)

Confirmed breaking events that cut across verticals. Primary source confirmation required before publication.

**Daily triggers:**
- Government announcements with technology, energy, or science implications
- Regulatory decisions (FCC, FTC, NRC, EU AI Act, FAA launch licensing, FERC grid orders)
- Major industrial events with technology or environmental dimensions
- Official policy documents, declassifications, or treaty developments

---

### 7. Engineering Analysis (Weekly / Long-form)

Deep technical analysis for practitioners: software engineers, systems architects, and technical founders. Not consumer explainers.

**Topics:**
- System design case studies at AI infrastructure scale
- Hardware architecture teardowns (GPU die analysis, packaging, memory subsystems)
- Infrastructure postmortems and lessons from large-scale failure events
- Quantum error correction progress reports and milestone analysis

---

### 8. Selective Coverage (High-value only, not daily)

**Neuroscience / Brain-Computer Interfaces:** Neuralink, Synchron, Blackrock — clinical trial data and hardware specs only. No lifestyle angle.

**Geopolitics of Technology:** CHIPS Act implementation, NVIDIA export controls, EU AI Act enforcement, ITU space debris governance. Cover on concrete regulatory decisions only, never as opinion.

**Hypersonics and Advanced Aviation:** Boom Supersonic, Hermeus, DARPA programs — on confirmed flight test results and technical disclosures only.

---

## What We Do NOT Cover

- Celebrity, lifestyle, or entertainment without a verified tech or science angle
- Cryptocurrency price prediction or trading advice
- Opinion without named expert sourcing
- Unverified leaks presented as fact
- Politics as a standalone topic
- Any story that cannot be sourced to a named primary document or named expert

## Today's Coverage Format

Every article published today follows this structure:

```
1. Opening paragraph — direct answer to what happened (2-4 sentences, primary source named)
2. Context paragraph — why it matters, who is affected
3. <h2> sections — specific sub-questions, data points, named sources, figures
4. <h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
   └── 5x <h3> questions with <p> answers
5. <SourcesInterlink> component — 4 external + 4 internal links
```

**Minimum requirements per article:**
- 300+ words
- 4+ internal OzoneNews links
- 1+ external primary source link
- `metadata.description`: 130-155 characters
- `metadata.title`: max 60 characters, format `Primary Keyword | Specific Detail`
- `published_at`: full ISO-8601 with timezone offset
- `thumbnail_src`: required for Google Top Stories eligibility
- Registry entry in `content/static/content_registry.json`
