
Sidemen Inside Season 3: Netflix Spring 2026 Release Confirmed with Full Cast and Production Details
The Sidemen have completed production on Inside UK Season 3, with Netflix confirming an exclusive spring 2026 premiere for the reality competition series. Filming wrapped in late 2025, following the rapid production cycle established in prior seasons. Multiple reports, including fan compilations and social media posts from official channels, point to March 23, 2026 as the most frequently cited release date .
Season 3 features 12 contestants living together for seven days in a house, competing in challenges that directly reduce or preserve a shared £1 million prize fund. The format retains core mechanics from Seasons 1 and 2, including temptation rooms, tuck shop spending, voting eliminations, and a final split-or-steal decision.

Netflix greenlit Season 3 following the strong performance of Season 2, which reached the UK Netflix Top 10 within days of its March 2025 debut .
Spring 2026 window aligns with the March release pattern seen in Season 2. No exact day has been published by Netflix, but March 23, 2026 appears consistently in fan tracking and secondary reports .

Core Format and Prize Mechanics
Contestants compete over seven days with the following mechanics:

Prize fund starts at £1 million — Deductions occur through spending or challenge failures.
Tuck Shop — Items purchased reduce the total prize pot.
Temptation Room — Solo access for 30 minutes; giving in to temptation lowers the fund.
Team and individual challenges — Success preserves or increases value; failure deducts.
Voting and eliminations — Periodic removals narrow the field.
Final two-player dilemma — Remaining contestants decide to split or steal the remaining amount.

These elements create strategic tension between cooperation and self-interest, as documented in prior seasons .
Production Under Sidemen Productions
Season 3 marks the first major project from Sidemen Productions, the newly formed company established by the Sidemen (Joshua Bradley, Tobi Brown, Simon Minter, Olajide Olatunji, Ethan Payne, Vikram Barn, Harry Lewis). Adam Cohen serves as Head of Sidemen Productions, with a new London headquarters under development .
Production was self-funded by the Sidemen, maintaining their focus on UK-centric content while positioning for broader international collaborations.
