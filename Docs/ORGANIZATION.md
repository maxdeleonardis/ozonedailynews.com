# ObjectWire | Organization Overview

**Last Updated: May 15, 2026**

---

## What ObjectWire Is

ObjectWire is a nonprofit investigative news organization and licensed private detective agency headquartered in Austin, Texas. We operate at the intersection of journalism, public accountability, and field investigation, offering services that most media companies cannot: we both report the story and investigate it.

We are not a blog. We are not an aggregator. We are not a for-profit media company. Every dollar ObjectWire generates is reinvested into our operations, our reporting, and the readers and communities we serve.

---

## Mission

To deliver verified, source-cited reporting and investigative services that protect individuals, expose wrongdoing, and advance public understanding, especially on topics that other outlets lack the expertise or resources to cover honestly.

We believe the public deserves:

- **Accurate information**, verified against primary sources
- **Transparent reporting**, where every claim is traceable
- **Access to investigation**, not just commentary about it
- **Scientific literacy**, not just science headlines

---

## Legal Structure

ObjectWire is a formally registered **501(c)(3) nonprofit organization**, incorporated in the State of Wyoming as **ObjectWire LLC**. We do not have shareholders, outside investors, or a profit-extraction motive. All revenue generated by ObjectWire, whether from PI service fees, subscriptions, or licensing, is returned to the organization to fund reporting, tools, staff, and reader services.

As a 501(c)(3), donations to ObjectWire may be tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law. Our tax-exempt status means editorial decisions are legally insulated from commercial pressure.

There is no outside entity with financial control over editorial decisions.

## Headquarters

**Austin, Texas**

ObjectWire is based in Austin, TX and operates under Texas state jurisdiction. Our licensed PI services are regulated under the **Texas Occupations Code, Chapter 1702**, enforced by the Texas Department of Public Safety.

We serve Austin, Travis County, and surrounding Central Texas areas for field investigation work. National and international reporting has no geographic restriction.

## What We Do

### 1. Investigative Journalism

ObjectWire publishes original reporting across the following verticals:

| Vertical | Coverage Focus |
|---|---|
| News | Breaking events, policy, government accountability |
| Tech | AI, software, hardware, platform companies |
| Finance | Markets, crypto, regulatory actions, fraud |
| Science | Biotech, medical research, physics, climate |
| Politics | Congress, elections, lobbying, public records |
| Entertainment | Studios, gaming, creator economy |
| NGOs | Nonprofit accountability, grant tracking, corruption |

Our reporting relies exclusively on primary sources: court filings, SEC disclosures, FOIA records, peer-reviewed studies, and named on-record sources. We do not rewrite competitor coverage.

### 2. Licensed PI Services (Austin, Texas)

ObjectWire operates a licensed private detective agency in Austin, Texas. Services include:

- **Surveillance** (domestic, corporate, insurance)
- **Infidelity Investigations**
- **Missing Persons and Skip Tracing**
- **Background Checks**
- **Child Custody and Family Law Support**
- **Workers Compensation Fraud**
- **Corporate Due Diligence**
- **Digital Forensics and Open Source Intelligence (OSINT)**
- **Evidence Collection for Legal Proceedings**

All PI work is conducted in compliance with Texas state law. Client confidentiality is absolute. We do not share case information with third parties without a court order or explicit written client consent.

Inquiry: [editorial@objectwire.org](mailto:editorial@objectwire.org) | Subject line: `PI Service Inquiry`

See: [objectwire.org/austin-private-detective-agency](https://www.objectwire.org/austin-private-detective-agency)

### 3. Scientific Expertise and Analysis

ObjectWire maintains in-house editorial expertise across a range of scientific disciplines. This is not generalist science journalism, it is domain-specific reporting by writers with academic and professional backgrounds in:

- **Biotech and Medical Research** (clinical trials, drug development, FDA processes)
- **AI and Machine Learning** (model architecture, training pipelines, policy implications)
- **Physics and Aerospace** (propulsion, orbital mechanics, materials science)
- **Environmental Science** (climate data, regulatory standards, pollution law)
- **Neuroscience and Psychology** (cognitive research, behavioral studies)
- **Cybersecurity** (vulnerability disclosure, threat intelligence, infrastructure attacks)

We hold science to the same sourcing standard as any other beat: primary studies, named researchers, institutional data. We do not amplify press releases without independent verification.

## How We Are Funded

ObjectWire is self-funded by its founding team and operating revenue. We do not accept:

- Advertising of any kind
- Sponsored content or paid placements
- Affiliate commissions
- Political donations or PAC money
- Government grants tied to editorial coverage

All operational surplus is reinvested in:

- Reporter salaries and freelance fees
- Investigative expenses (travel, FOIA filings, legal review)
- Platform infrastructure and development
- Reader tools (free access, search, notifications)
- PI service equipment and licensing

**We exist to serve readers, not extract value from them.**

## Editorial Principles

1. **Accuracy over speed.** We verify before publishing. We do not chase breaking news without sourcing.
2. **Primary sources only.** Every factual claim traces to an origin document, not an aggregator.
3. **Transparent corrections.** Errors are corrected publicly, timestamped, with the original text preserved.
4. **Clear labeling.** News and analysis are always labeled separately. Opinion carries a byline and disclosure.
5. **Named authors.** Every article carries a byline linked to a public author profile.
6. **No conflicts.** No writer covers a company, fund, or individual they have a financial relationship with.

Full standards: [objectwire.org/editorial-standards](https://www.objectwire.org/editorial-standards)
